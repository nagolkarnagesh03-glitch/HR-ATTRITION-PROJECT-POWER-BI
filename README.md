# HR-ATTRITION-PROJECT-POWER-BI

# 📊 HR Analytics: Employee Attrition & Performance Dashboard

## 🧾 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition and performance using an interactive dashboard built in Power BI. The objective is to identify key factors influencing employee turnover and provide actionable insights to improve workforce retention and organizational performance.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition trends across different demographics
* Identify key drivers of employee turnover
* Evaluate the impact of salary, overtime, and tenure on attrition
* Provide insights to support HR decision-making

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies

* Power BI (Dashboard Development)
* Microsoft Excel (Data Cleaning & Preparation)
* DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)

---

## 📁 Dataset Description

The dataset contains employee-related information such as:

* Employee ID
* Age
* Gender
* Department
* Job Role
* Salary
* Years at Company
* Years Since Last Promotion
* Job Level
* Overtime Status
* Attrition (Yes/No)

---

# 📌 Dashboard Structure

---

## 🟢 Page 1: Attrition Overview

### 📊 Key KPIs

* Total Employees: 1470
* Attrition Rate: 16.08%
* Average Salary: 6.50K
* Average Tenure: 7.01 Years
* Average Hike: 15.21%

---

### 📈 Visualizations

* Attrition Rate by Age Group
* Attrition Distribution by Gender
* Attrition by Marital Status
* Attrition Rate by Job Role
* Attrition Rate by Department

---

### 🔍 Key Insights

* Overall attrition rate stands at **16.08%**, indicating moderate employee turnover
* Employees aged **18–25 have the highest attrition (~35%)**
* **Sales department shows the highest attrition (~20.67%)**
* **Sales Representatives have the highest attrition (~39%) among job roles**
* **Single employees contribute the highest attrition (~52%)**
* Attrition is slightly higher among male employees

---

---

## 🔵 Page 2: Attrition Drivers & Insights

### 📊 Visualizations

* Attrition Rate by Salary Range
* Attrition by Overtime Status
* Attrition by Years Since Last Promotion
* Attrition by Tenure (Years at Company)
* Attrition by Job Level

---

### 🔍 Key Insights

* Employees with **low salary (≤ 5K)** show the highest attrition (~21.65%)
* **Employees working overtime contribute significantly to attrition (~74%)**
* Highest attrition observed among employees with **0–2 years since last promotion**
* Employees with **short tenure (0–2 years)** have the highest attrition
* **Job Level 1 employees show highest attrition (~26%)**, indicating entry-level instability
